About the Author

Danial Jiwani, the author, is currently 17 years old, and he is passionate about stock market investing. He has spent the last couple of years learning about strategies the successful investors use to beat the stock market at its own game. Using a long-term, value investing strategy, he is able to make money like Warren Buffett. As an expert in investing, he teaches other new investors the fundamentals of investing. He is also currently one of the youngest authors who writes books about business. Danial has also taught many students about microeconomics in his book called Surviving AP Econ: Microeconomics. Through his books, Danial is always sharing knowledge with the youth of the world.

5.0 out of 5 stars Excellent book for a new investor
Reviewed in the United States on August 11, 2019
An excellent book for a beginning investor, this book is written in langue fit for any aspiring investor. By going step by step through the basics and strategies of investing, the author teaches investing in a concise and detailed fashion. The author also explains the best investing strategies for a beginner while giving reasoning for following the suggested strategies.

In the subsequent chapters the author discusses the role of the analyst and what to look for in the analyst report. He also talks about the dangers of relying on the analyst reports. The author also discusses the advantage of diversification and how small investors can diversify their portfolio. In the next chapter the author discusses the role of the dividend and pros & con of investing in companies that pay dividend. The author also introduces the reader to fundamental analytics found on Yahoo Finance and gives a simple explanation of some of the analytics. He explains what to look for in the 10-K report. In the later chapters the author discusses the ideal number of stocks to own, the best time to buy and sell the stocks.

An excellent work for novice investors, this book is especially valuable for high school, college students and anyone who is new to investing. This should be the first book read by a beginning investor.

HussainFormer JP Morgan Research Analyst
